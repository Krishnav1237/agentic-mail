# Launch Guide

Inbox Intelligence Layer should be launched with a general productivity angle:

> Your inbox generates work. IIL turns that work into structured execution.

That framing matches the public landing page and keeps the product broad enough
for personal Gmail and Outlook users dealing with leads, deadlines, follow-ups,
approvals, and overloaded inboxes.

## What The Waitlist Page Promises

- Your inbox runs itself
- Personal Gmail and Outlook support
- Task extraction, smart replies, scheduling, and follow-ups
- Approval controls and privacy-first execution

Use those same promises in launch posts so the traffic and the waitlist page
stay aligned.

## Launch Assets To Prepare

Keep the first launch simple:

- one 20-30 second screen recording
- one before/after static screenshot
- one concise one-line value proposition with the waitlist link

The current landing page supports these proof points:

- email in -> structured tasks out
- full-thread context -> draft ready
- low-signal inbox noise -> high-signal view
- opportunities and deadlines -> tracked automatically

## Platform Playbook

### X

- Lead with the problem, not the brand name
- Use one founder post, one short thread, and one short product clip
- Pin the strongest post to the profile
- Spend the first week replying to people already talking about inbox overload,
  follow-ups, missed deadlines, and admin work

Suggested hook:

> Your inbox is a task manager pretending to be an email client. I built IIL to turn incoming email into tasks, deadlines, drafts, and follow-ups automatically.

### Instagram

- Use visual proof, not feature lists
- Post one Reel, one carousel, and Story link stickers to the waitlist
- Show concrete flows: email arrives, task appears, reply draft appears
- Keep captions direct and outcome-focused

Suggested hook:

> If your inbox creates work, why are you still converting it manually?

### Reddit

- Post as a builder asking for feedback
- Read each subreddit rules page before posting
- Keep links secondary to the discussion itself
- Stay active in comments after posting

Suggested angle:

> I am building a tool that turns email into tasks, replies, and follow-ups. What inbox workflows do you still do manually?

### WhatsApp

- Use it as a warm distribution channel first
- Send one short clip, one one-line explanation, and one direct link
- Focus on trusted contacts, alumni groups, founder circles, and small work
  groups

Suggested message:

> Built something for people whose inbox keeps turning into a to-do list. Would love your honest take.

### Discord

- Prefer showing the product inside existing communities first
- Ask mods before posting demos in other servers
- If you open your own server later, keep it simple:
  - one announcements channel
  - one waitlist or feedback forum
  - one use-cases forum for replies, deadlines, opportunities, and bugs

## Suggested Sequence

### Day 1

- X founder launch
- Instagram Reel
- WhatsApp founder blast

### Day 2

- Discord demos in relevant communities

### Day 3-5

- Reddit feedback posts where rules allow them

### Week 2

- Publish learnings from the waitlist
- Share what people want automated most
- Post product changes, not just launch reminders

## Pre-Launch Check

- Landing page metadata matches the general productivity positioning
- Shared links render a title, description, and image correctly
- Waitlist submit works end-to-end with Supabase Edge Functions
- Duplicate signups return the duplicate message without resending email
- Resend sender moves off the default once a verified domain is ready
